Survivor

 

         Millions of people in the  U.S. have watched a new summer television show called “Survivor.”  “Survivor” turned out to be a game, where being smart was rewarded with $1 million at the end.  But along the way, many ethical issues came up.

 

         For example, imagine that you are in a shipwreck.  You swim to a small island along with some other shipmates.  It’s very difficult to survive.  Some may live and some may die.   If you do the things that are best for you, it may make life harder for someone else.  Do you take care of yourself and not think about others?  Have you ever been in a “survival” or crisis situation?  Describe such a case to your partners.

 

         We sometimes answer the question, “How are you?” with “I’m surviving”.  If your friend says that to you, what do you think he or she means?  Do you use this term?  Even though you are not living on a desert island, do you sometimes feel that you have to work hard just to survive life?  Give examples.  What have you “survived” so far in your life?

 

         The final survivor on the TV show won a million dollars.  If you won a million dollars, what would you do with it?  How would it affect your life?  Would you compromise your principles of what is good and right in order to win $1million dollars?  Why or why not?

 

         We use the idiom, “giving our word” to mean that what we say can be trusted.  Suppose I promise my boyfriend, “while you’re gone for a year, I will not find another boyfriend or love another.”  I can say to him, “I give you my word.”  It’s not written in a contract.  But my word is good.  My word can be trusted.  Or if I break my promise and find another boyfriend, I am “breaking my word.”  Do you have this concept of “giving your word” or “breaking your word” in your native country?  Give examples of how this would be used, or of an equivalent way to make a promise.

 

         What are your favorite types of indoor games?  Some examples in the U.S. are word games (Scrabble, Balderdash, Taboo), board games (Monopoly, Sorry, Scotland Yard, Chess, Checkers, Backgammon), card games (Bridge, Hearts, Pounce, Crazy Eights, Daifugo), political strategy games (Risk, Diplomacy).   Do you prefer games of chance or games of skill?  Give examples of games that are mostly skill, mostly chance or a mixture of both.   What kind of games do you like, if any?  Do you have difficulty agreeing on a game to play in your family?

 

         What were your favorite games as a child, indoor or outdoor?  How important was “fair play?”

 

 

 

         God arranged a “survivor” type test for the nation of Israel when He led them into the desert after he freed them from Egypt.   Moses told the people, “Don’t forget what you learned in the desert.”  He said,

 

         “And you shall remember all the way which the Lord your God has led you in the desert these forty years, that He might humble you, testing you, to know what was in your heart, whether you would keep His commandments or not.  And He humbled you and let you be hungry, and fed you with manna which you did not know, not did your fathers know, that He might make you understand that man does not live by bread alone, but by everything that comes from the mouth of God.”  Deuteronomy 8:2-3
